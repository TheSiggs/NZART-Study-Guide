7Power Calculations

As with ohms law, the power law can be read from the triangle above. E = Potential Difference (Volts), P = Power (Watts), I = Current (Amps).

P = E × I
E = P ÷ I
I = P ÷ E

Learn the above triangle and remember it.

Example 1. A transmitter power amplifier requires 30 mA at 300 V. Calculate the DC input power. We know E and I, and thus need to calculate P.

P = E × I
= 300 × 0.03
= 9W

Example 2. The current in a 100 kΩ resistor is 10 mA. What power (heat) is the resistor dissipating? We know R = 100000 and I = 0.01.

Step 1 - We have I and R. We can find E using ohms law.

E = I × R
= 0.01 × 100000
= 1000V

Step 2 - Now we have E and I, we can find P.

P = E × I
= 1000 × 0.01
= 10W

Example 3. Two 10Ω resistors are connected in series with a 10V battery supplying current. Find the total power load.

Step 1 — Find RT for a series network

RT = R1 + R2
= 10 + 10
= 20Ω

Step 2 — Find I using ohms law

I = E / R
= 10 / 20
= 0.5A

Step 3 — Find P using the power law

P = E × I
= 10 × 0.5
= 5W
