Q CODES

These abbreviated three letter “Q” Codes were evolved by old-time telegraphy operators as a shorthand means for exchanging information about working conditions being experienced over the circuit in use.

You will be tested on only 10 of the 40 or so Q Code messages that are used in amateur radio communication.

Many can be used as a query if followed by a question mark, e. g. QRM? QTH? or as an answer to a query or as a statement of fact with no question mark, e.g. QTH Auckland, QTH San Francisco etc.

All Q codes may be used while operating CW and some are used during phone transmissions.

Q Code Meaning Notes
QRL?Are you busyCommonly means “is the frequency in use?” [25.6]
QRMYour transmission is being interfered with[25.1]
QRNI am troubled by static[25.2]
QRP?Shall I decrease transmitter power?Without query: “I am running low power” [25.7]
QRQPlease send faster[25.10]
QRSPlease send slowerWith query: “shall I (or we) send slower?” [25.3]
QRZ?Who is calling me?Commonly “who is on this frequency?” if unable to copy callsign [25.4]
QSBYour signals are fadingAs part of a signal report [25.8]
QSY?Shall I change to transmission on another frequency?Without query: “I am going to change frequency/up 5 (kHZ)/ to 28.459 etc.” [25.9]
QTH?What is your location?Without query: “QTH Melbourne” means “my location is Melbourne” [25.5]

You will need to memorize these Q Codes before the course starts

Hints

  • Often QRM and QRN are confused
  • QRM is Man made interference
  • QRN is Natural Noise
  • QRQ for Quicker
  • QRS for Slower
QQuestion File 25 – Q Codes (10 questions)

1. The signal "QRM" means:

  • ayour signals are fading
  • bI am troubled by static
  • cyour transmission is being interfered with
  • dis my transmission being interfered with?
Answer: C

2. The signal "QRN" means:

  • aI am busy
  • bI am troubled by static
  • care you troubled by static?
  • dI am being interfered with
Answer: B

3. The "Q signal" requesting the other station to send slower is:

  • aQRL
  • bQRN
  • cQRM
  • dQRS
Answer: D

4. The question "Who is calling me?" is asked by:

  • aQRT?
  • bQRM?
  • cQRP?
  • dQRZ?
Answer: D

5. The "Q" signal "what is your location?" is:

  • aQTH?
  • bQTC?
  • cQRL?
  • dQRZ?
Answer: A

6. The "Q" signal "are you busy?" is:

  • aQRM?
  • bQRL?
  • cQRT?
  • dQRZ?
Answer: B

7. The "Q" signal "shall I decrease transmitter power?" is:

  • aQRP?
  • bQRZ?
  • cQRN?
  • dQRL?
Answer: A

8. The "Q" signal "your signals are fading" is:

  • aQSO
  • bQSB
  • cQSL
  • dQRX
Answer: B

9. The signal "QSY?" means:

  • ashall I change to transmission on another frequency?
  • bshall I increase transmitter power?
  • cshall I relay to .... ?
  • dis my signal fading?

10. The "Q" signal which means "send faster" is:

  • aQRP
